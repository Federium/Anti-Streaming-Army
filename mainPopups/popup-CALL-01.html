Encrypted Call [10012018] - Transcript #1

Participants: Ronald Jefferson (Director, Streaming Lobby) & Dr. Zhao Ming (Director, Wuhan Laboratory)

Ronald Jefferson

[Distorted voice] Dr. Zhao, I assume you’ve reviewed the proposal and the terms we sent over?

I have, Mr. Jefferson. And I must say, the scope of what you’re asking is... extraordinary. To weaponize a virus for such purposes—this isn’t standard research. It’s dangerous.

Extraordinary, yes. But isn’t that what science is about? Pushing boundaries? Besides, the terms are clear. You and your team receive unparalleled funding and anonymity. The results, Dr. Zhao, will speak for themselves.

And if something goes wrong? A project of this scale carries enormous risks. The spread could be uncontrollable, and the fallout—

We’ve accounted for every scenario. The agreement we sent outlines contingency measures, doesn’t it? If you’re worried about liability, the contract protects you. And as for control, you’ll ensure the virus functions exactly as we’ve specified.

You make it sound simple. But biology is rarely predictable. Even with the best designs, nature finds a way to circumvent our intentions.

That’s why you’re the best in the field, Dr. Zhao. We trust you to refine it until it’s perfect. This isn’t your first groundbreaking achievement, after all. Consider the legacy this will leave behind.

Ming Legacy? This kind of legacy comes at a cost. I’ve reviewed the draft contract, and while the funding is substantial, it’s not just about money. My team’s safety, the lab’s reputation—those are at stake.

The contract isn’t just about compensation. It’s a guarantee. Full immunity, financial and legal. Your name, your lab—they stay out of the headlines. Any trace of involvement? Gone.

I’ve also reviewed the technical specifications you sent. You want a virus with high transmissibility, mild to moderate symptoms, and a delayed incubation period to maximize spread. It’s... possible. My team has already seen promising results in early simulations.

Excellent. That’s what I needed to hear. Once you finalize the virus, we’ll begin the next phase. As outlined in the agreement, the initial outbreak must look natural—animal-to-human transmission.

Necessary? Absolutely. People are complacent. Fear is the only motivator that works on a global scale. And our partners in media are ready to amplify that fear.

Very well. I’ll sign the contract and move forward, but I want regular updates and control over the initial release. If anything deviates, I’ll pull the plug.

Of course, Dr. Zhao. Your concerns are noted. But remember, this partnership is built on trust. We need each other to make this work.
Dr. Zhao Ming

[Quietly] Trust, indeed.
Ronald Jefferson

Once you’ve signed the contract, send it back through the encrypted channel. Our legal team will take care of the rest. Let’s not waste any more time. The world is ready for this... shift.

I’ll have it returned within 24 hours.
Ronald Jefferson

Good. History is in the making, Dr. Zhao. Don’t forget that.
